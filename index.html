About

I am Dr. Chirag Dhara, a climate and sustainability scientist currently serving as an Assistant Professor of Environmental Studies at Krea University in India. My work spans the physical science of climate change, its implications for policy, and, increasingly, its iomplications for human development on a finite and constrained planet.

My academic path is somewhat unconventional. I began my career as a theoretical physicist probing the role of randomness in the quantum world: the degree to which it exists and could be quantified. An absence of intrinsic randomness in the quantum realm would mean an absence of human free will! With this work, I earned my first Ph.D. in Quantum Information and Communication from the Institute of Photonic Sciences in Barcelona, Spain in 2013.

However, my longstanding interest in geopolitics, justice, and human rights led me to becoming increasingly invested into the escalating climate and ecological crises. I pivoted my research focus and pursued a second Ph.D. in Earth System Science at the Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry in Germany that I completed in 2017. I worked on simplfied models to probe the climate and hydrologic sensitivity from a surface energy perspective.

Before joining Krea, I was a Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology (IITM) in Pune. During this time, I was one of the co-authors of India's first comprehensive regional climate change assessment (WG1) report - a critical milestone in unconvering climate change trends over the subcontinent. I also served as a contributing author to Atlas in IPCC AR6 (2021).

In recent years, I have increasingly engaged in public outreach to convey implications from climate and sustainability research for society. I often explore themes that otherwise receive little attention in the media - for instance, implications of climate change for democracy, its exacerbation of war-inflation and implications for basic food security, etc. I have contributed to Scientific American, The Hindu, The Indian Express, Times of India, Hindustan Times, Mongabay India, Down to Earth, The India Forum, 360info, among others.
