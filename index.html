<section id="about">

    <h2>Profile</h2>

    <p>I am a scientist driven by the challenge of reconciling human development with planetary boundaries. With a unique foundation of dual doctorates—one in <strong>Quantum Information</strong> (ICFO, Spain) and another in <strong>Earth System Science</strong> (Max Planck Institute for Biogeochemistry, Germany)—I approach climate challenges with both rigorous mathematical modeling and a deep understanding of ecological limits.</p>

    <p>Currently serving as an Assistant Professor at Krea University, my work bridges the gap between hard physical science and socio-economic realities. My research spans atmospheric thermodynamics, the impact of aerosols on the Indian Monsoon, and critiques of traditional, GDP-centric development metrics. Ultimately, I aim to make climate science a tool for public empowerment, equity, and scalable sustainability.</p>

</section>

                <span class="pub-venue">Climate Policy (2025)</span>

            </div>

        </summary>

        <div class="accessible-summary">

            <strong>The Gist:</strong> Popular "green" rankings often unfairly penalize developing nations while rewarding wealthy countries that actually cause more per-capita emissions. We unpack why these metrics are mathematically and ethically skewed, and propose ways to measure climate action that are actually fair to the Global South.

            <div>Future intensification of Northern Hemisphere monsoons due to declining remote aerosol pollution

                <span class="pub-venue">Environmental Research Letters (2025)</span>

            </div>

        </summary>

        <div class="accessible-summary">

            <strong>The Gist:</strong> Cleaning up air pollution is good for human health, but it has a hidden global side effect. As North America and Europe reduce their "aerosol" pollution, the Northern Hemisphere warms faster. Our research shows this shifting heat imbalance is projected to significantly intensify India's monsoon rains in the future.

            <br><a href="pdfs/monsoons-aerosols-2025.pdf" class="btn" target="_blank">Download PDF</a>
